ICLR 2023 sets a new record with 192 physics-related papers, showing physics principles are now fundamental to machine learning. Diffusion models reach full maturity with 27 papers, equivariant models continue dominance with 39 papers, and every physics category shows substantial representation. This represents a 1029% increase from 2013.

Compared to previous years:
- New record: 192 papers (51.2% growth)
- Diffusion models peak at 27 papers (14.1%)
- Equivariant models reach 39 papers (20.3%)
- Variational methods show resurgence (15 papers)
- PDEs/Physics-informed solidify (11 papers)
- Molecular applications stabilize (14 papers)
- "Other" category largest ever (61 papers) showing breadth
