ICLR 2015 marks a pivotal year with the introduction of key optimization methods (Adam optimizer) and adversarial approaches. While total physics-related papers slightly decreased, the impact and sophistication of contributions increased significantly.

Compared to previous years:
- Introduction of Adam optimizer - a landmark contribution
- First adversarial methods appear
- Continued theoretical analysis of deep networks
- Slight decrease in total papers but higher impact contributions
- Growing computational focus (FFT methods, CP-decomposition)

* Key Insights for 2015
- Adam optimizer introduced - becomes dominant optimization method
- Adversarial examples first studied systematically
- Energy-based models show theoretical depth (universal approximation)
- Computational efficiency becomes a theme (FFT, decomposition methods)
- Variational methods: 22.2% (down from 36.8% in 2014)
- Stochastic methods maintain steady presence at 22.2%
