#+TITLE: Neurosymbolic AI Papers (ICML, ICLR, NeurIPS 2024–2025)

1. *Learning to grok: Emergence of in-context learning and skill composition in modular arithmetic tasks*
   - Venue: NeurIPS 2024 (Oral)
   - Authors: Tianyu He et al.
   - Summary: Observes /phase-transition-like behavior/ in modular arithmetic tasks. Transformers exhibit a sharp generalization shift after overfitting, reminiscent of phase transitions.
   - Physics aspect: Emergent phase behavior.
   - Neurosymbolic aspect: Implicit finite automaton discovery by transformers.

2. *Arrows of Time for Large Language Models*
   - Venue: ICML 2024 (Oral)
   - Authors: Vassilis Papadopoulos, Jérémie Wenger, Clement Hongler
   - Summary: Explores /temporal asymmetry/ in LLMs, identifying a directional bias in sequence learning akin to the /arrow of time/ in physics.
   - Physics aspect: Entropy and time asymmetry.
   - Neurosymbolic aspect: Symbolic representations affected by temporal bias.

3. *Emergence of meta-stable clustering in mean-field transformer models*
   - Venue: ICLR 2025 (Oral)
   - Authors: Giuseppe Bruno, Federico Pasqualotto, Andrea Agazzi
   - Summary: Models token dynamics via /mean-field PDE/, revealing meta-stable clustering analogous to physical phases.
   - Physics aspect: Phase transitions, meta-stability.
   - Neurosymbolic aspect: Mean-field abstraction of transformer behavior.

4. *Brain Bandit: A Biologically Grounded Neural Network for Efficient Control of Exploration*
   - Venue: ICLR 2025 (Oral)
   - Authors: Chen Jiang et al.
   - Summary: Uses /stochastic Hopfield networks/ to simulate posterior sampling. Efficient bandit strategy arises from spin-glass-like dynamics.
   - Physics aspect: Hopfield–Ising model analogy.
   - Neurosymbolic aspect: Sampling-based reasoning via biologically inspired neural circuits.

5. *KAN: Kolmogorov–Arnold Networks*
   - Venue: ICLR 2025 (Oral)
   - Authors: Ziming Liu et al.
   - Summary: New architecture where each "weight" is a /learnable univariate function/, inspired by /Kolmogorov–Arnold representation/.
   - Physics aspect: Decomposition of multivariate functions.
   - Neurosymbolic aspect: Explicit symbolic decomposition via learnable nonlinear edges.

6. *Artificial Kuramoto Oscillatory Neurons (AKOrN)*
   - Venue: ICLR 2025 (Oral)
   - Authors: Takeru Miyato, Sindy Löwe, Andreas Geiger, Max Welling
   - Summary: Introduces neurons based on the /Kuramoto model/, enabling /phase synchronization/ for concept abstraction.
   - Physics aspect: Coupled oscillator dynamics.
   - Neurosymbolic aspect: Binding mechanism for compositional reasoning.

7. *Algorithm Development in Neural Networks: Insights from the Streaming Parity Task*
   - Venue: ICML 2025 (Oral)
   - Authors: Loek van Rossem, Andrew Saxe
   - Summary: RNNs undergo a /phase transition/ from overfitting to infinite generalization as data increases, mimicking automata learning.
   - Physics aspect: Finite-size effects, sudden generalization jumps.
   - Neurosymbolic aspect: Implicit construction of symbolic automata.

8. *Learning Energy Decompositions for Partial Inference in GFlowNets*
   - Venue: ICLR 2024 (Oral)
   - Authors: Hyosoon Jang, Minsu Kim, Sungsoo Ahn
   - Summary: Decomposes /Boltzmann energies/ over generation steps in GFlowNets for more efficient structured sampling.
   - Physics aspect: Energy-based modeling, Boltzmann sampling.
   - Neurosymbolic aspect: Hybrid energy-decomposed symbolic generation.

9. *An Analytic Theory of Creativity in Convolutional Diffusion Models*
   - Venue: ICML 2025 (Oral)
   - Authors: Mason Kamb, Surya Ganguli
   - Summary: Uses /mean-field theory/ to explain creativity as /patch mosaic recombination/ in convnet diffusion models.
   - Physics aspect: Statistical mosaic phase theory.
   - Neurosymbolic aspect: Structural inductive biases for combinatorial novelty.

10. *LSEnet: Lorentz Structural Entropy Neural Network for Deep Graph Clustering*
    - Venue: ICML 2024 (Oral)
    - Authors: Li Sun et al.
    - Summary: Minimizes /structural entropy/ in hyperbolic space for unsupervised graph clustering.
    - Physics aspect: Entropy minimization in Lorentzian geometry.
    - Neurosymbolic aspect: Symbolic entropy objective in graph learning.

11. *Grokking as a First Order Phase Transition in Two Layer Networks*
    - Venue: ICLR 2024 (Spotlight)
    - Authors: Noa Rubin, Inbar Seroussi, Zohar Ringel
    - Summary: Grokking is modeled as a /first-order transition/ in internal network states, formalized via adaptive kernel theory.
    - Physics aspect: Order parameters and discontinuous learning regimes.
    - Neurosymbolic aspect: Transition to symbolic rule representation.

12. *Sudden Drops in the Loss: Syntax Acquisition, Phase Transitions, and Simplicity Bias in MLMs*
    - Venue: ICLR 2024 (Spotlight)
    - Authors: Angelica Chen, Ravid Shwartz-Ziv, Kyunghyun Cho et al.
    - Summary: Syntax in MLMs emerges abruptly alongside loss drops.
    - Physics aspect: Order parameters emerge discontinuously.
    - Neurosymbolic aspect: Syntactic structure as symbolic latent variable.

13. *Scalable Discrete Diffusion Samplers: Combinatorial Optimization and Statistical Physics*
    - Venue: ICLR 2025 (Poster)
    - Authors: Sebastian Sanokowski et al.
    - Summary: Efficient discrete diffusion modeling for /Ising and combinatorial problems/.
    - Physics aspect: Sampling from Gibbs distributions.
    - Neurosymbolic aspect: Learned samplers for symbolic optimization domains.

14. *Grokking in Linear Estimators – A Solvable Model that Groks without Understanding*
    - Venue: ICLR 2024 (Poster)
    - Authors: Hugo Cui, Freya Behrens, Florent Krzakala, Lenka Zdeborová
    - Summary: Even linear models can grok due to optimization dynamics.
    - Physics aspect: Analytic modeling of grokking as a phase change.
    - Neurosymbolic aspect: Emergence of symbolic solutions in minimal models.

15. *Phase Transition between Positional and Semantic Learning in Deep Networks*
    - Venue: NeurIPS 2024 (Spotlight)
    - Authors: Hugo Cui et al.
    - Summary: ConvNets transition from positional cue use to /semantic abstraction/ in learning.
    - Physics aspect: Gardner-style phase transitions.
    - Neurosymbolic aspect: Learning shifts from superficial to symbolic.
