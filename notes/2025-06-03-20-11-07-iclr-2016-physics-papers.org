ICLR 2016 shows explosive growth in physics-inspired approaches with the introduction of GANs to the physics community, explicit physics applications (billiards), and sophisticated variational methods. This year marks the beginning of rapid expansion in the field.

Compared to previous years:
- Largest year-over-year growth so far (+38.9%)
- First explicit physics simulation paper (billiards)
- GANs enter the physics-ML intersection
- Gaussian Processes emerge as a distinct category
- Compression and efficiency become major themes
- Introduction of continuous control with deep RL
