This inaugural ICLR conference establishes foundational physics-inspired approaches in deep learning, with strong emphasis on statistical mechanics (Boltzmann machines, Gibbs sampling) and matrix factorization methods. The conference shows early adoption of physics principles for optimization and sampling.

As the first year in our analysis, ICLR 2013 sets the baseline for physics-inspired machine learning research. Key themes that will persist throughout the decade are already visible: energy-based models, variational methods, and stochastic optimization.
