* Suggested Mood Bins
** 1. Energetic/Heroic
- Music that feels driving, propulsive, or grandly assertive. Often good for working out, staying motivated, or focusing intensely.
- Typical examples in Beethoven: Symphony No. 3 ("Eroica"), Symphony No. 5, Symphony No. 7, Overtures such as "Coriolan" & "Egmont," the "Appassionata" Sonata, "Hammerklavier" Sonata, "Kreutzer" Sonata, Piano Concerto No. 5 ("Emperor"), etc.

** 2. Dramatic/Intense
- Similar energy to "Heroic," but sometimes darker, with more conflict or weight. Good for powerful emotional engagement.
- Typical examples: Symphony No. 9 (some movements), "Coriolan" Overture, "Pathétique" Sonata, late string quartets with intense contrasts, Grosse Fuge, "Serioso" Quartet.

** 3. Bright/Lyrical
- Light, singing, often cheerful or gracefully classical. Good for a positive lift without the bombast.
- Typical examples: Symphony Nos. 1 & 2, the "Spring" Violin Sonata (No. 5), many early piano sonatas, various chamber works for flute or winds.

** 4. Serene/Reflective
- Calmer, more contemplative or pastoral in character. Good for relaxing, reading, or unwinding.
- Typical examples: Symphony No. 6 ("Pastoral"), certain middle piano sonatas ("Pastoral" Sonata Op. 28), the slow movements of late string quartets.

** 5. Spiritual/Profound
- Especially the late works (late piano sonatas, late string quartets, Missa Solemnis), which can be meditative, otherworldly, or transcendently emotional.
- Typical examples: Piano Sonatas Nos. 30--32, String Quartets Nos. 12--16, Missa Solemnis.

** 6. Lighthearted/Playful
- Music that's dance-like, witty, or designed for social occasions.
- Typical examples: Dances & Marches (German Dances, Contredanses), many small variations, some early serenades.

** 7. Folkish/Eclectic
- Beethoven's arrangements of folk songs or smaller-scale curiosities, combining a lighter spirit with local color.
- Typical examples: Scottish/Irish/Welsh folksong settings, various minor WoO works, canons, or musical jokes.

* Especially "Energetic" Picks
If you specifically want activity-/motivation-oriented Beethoven, look for any CD labeled Energetic/Heroic, Fiery/Intense, or Exuberant/Dramatic. Here are some of the most obvious "high-energy" highlights (not an exhaustive list, but a quick reference):

- *Symphonic Energetic*:
  - CD1, CD2, CD3, CD4, CD5 (modern instrument cycle)
  - CD7, CD8 (Vienna cycle with #3, #5, #7)
  - CD12, CD13, CD14, CD15 (period instruments, featuring 3, 5, 7, 9)
- *Heroic Piano Works*:
  - CD18 (Piano Concerto #5 "Emperor")
  - CD22 (another take on #5)
  - CD37, CD38, CD39, CD42 (Sonatas "Waldstein," "Appassionata," "Hammerklavier")
- *Fiery Chamber*:
  - CD54 (Violin Sonatas #7 & #9 "Kreutzer")
  - CD72 (String Quartet #11 "Serioso," #13 + Grosse Fuge)
- *Historic Recordings*:
  - CD102 (Famous conductors' takes on #3, #5, #7, #9)
  - CD103, CD104 (Furtwängler, Kleiber's intense #5)

Whenever you see "Symphony #5," "Eroica," "Seventh," "Appassionata," "Kreutzer," "Emperor," "Waldstein," or "Hammerklavier," you're in that prime Beethoven energy zone.

Enjoy exploring these "mood bins," and remember that many Beethoven works (especially multi-movement pieces) will include both stormy, intense sections and gentler, reflective moments---so these labels are only an overall guide!
