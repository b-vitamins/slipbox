* Suggested Mozart Mood Bins
** 1. Energetic/Exuberant
- Music that's upbeat, bustling, or joyously propulsive---perfect for motivation, "getting things done," or single-minded focus. Often in major keys with brilliant, sparkling orchestration or keyboard writing.

** 2. Dramatic/Intense
- Works that incorporate minor keys, sharper emotional contrasts, turbulence, or pathos. Good for emotional engagement, a sense of urgency, or gravitas.

** 3. Bright/Lyrical
- Light, singing, and melodious---often graceful yet not merely "background." Good for a positive lift, with that inimitable Mozartean clarity.

** 4. Serene/Reflective
- Calmer, sometimes introspective or pastoral. Works that soothe or invite contemplative listening (whether in slow movements or entire compositions).

** 5. Spiritual/Profound
- Sacred music (Masses, Requiem, Vespers) or other works radiating solemnity, reverence, or transcendent depth.

** 6. Lighthearted/Playful
- Music written for social occasions (serenades, divertimenti, many early symphonies and dances), or pieces with a distinctly witty, entertaining spirit.

** 7. Operatic/Theatrical
- All of Mozart's stage works (opera seria, opera buffa, singspiel, serenatas), plus certain dramatic arrangements. Immersive storytelling in music, from comedic to tragic.

** 8. Eclectic/Various
- Fragments, sketches, completions, or arrangements whose character can range widely. Often a grab-bag of curiosities that don't fit squarely into one of the above "pure" moods.

Note: Some multi-work CDs inevitably contain pieces spanning different moods; the assignment below reflects either the dominant mood or the most characteristic piece(s) on that disc.

* How to Use These Buckets
** If you're seeking energizing, motivating Mozart
  Look first to Energetic/Exuberant (Symphonies 31, 35, 36, "Jeunehomme" Concerto K271, 2-Piano Concerto K365, etc.) or the "major" large-scale works that sparkle and drive forward. Some Dramatic/Intense pieces (like Symphony No. 40, Piano Concerto No. 24) can also supercharge a deep-focus session---but they bring a more serious edge.
** For a general sense of refined, graceful Mozart
  Explore Bright/Lyrical or Lighthearted/Playful categories, especially the divertimenti, many mid-period concertos, or the typical "sunny" chamber works.
** For sacred solace or deeper contemplation
  The Spiritual/Profound sets (Masses, Vespers, the Requiem) are unmatched in their solemn beauty.
** For Opera lovers
The Operatic/Theatrical sets will make you discover the full range of comedic sparkle, emotional confrontation, heartbreak, and joy that only Mozart's stage works can deliver.

* Quick Reference by Bucket
- *Energetic/Exuberant*
  CD17 (K448, etc.), CD19 (K526), CD57, CD58, CD59, CD77, CD79, CD88, CD91

- *Dramatic/Intense*
  CDs featuring minor-key sonatas/quartets (e.g. CD4, CD7, CD15, CD26, CD28, CD29, etc.)
  Key symphonies #25, #40 (CD54, CD60, etc.) and many discs containing those works
  Piano concertos K466, K491, Sinfonia Concertante K364, Fantasias K475/K397, etc.

- *Bright/Lyrical*
  Many early-to-mid piano sonatas (CD2, CD5, CD6...), violin concertos, early piano concertos, and so forth
  A large swath of chamber music in major keys (e.g. K296, K376, etc.)

- *Serene/Reflective*
  CDs that highlight the more introspective or intimate side (CD20 "Kegelstatt," CD87 with K622, etc.)

- *Spiritual/Profound*
  CD153--CD170 (Masses, Requiem), plus the Handel oratorio arrangements (CD184--CD187), Masonic music (CD172), and spurious masses on CD200

- *Lighthearted/Playful*
  Nearly all Divertimenti/Serenades (CD35--CD39, CD62--CD73), Dances (CD69--CD73), many early symphonies (CD50--CD53, CD56), early violin sonatas

- *Operatic/Theatrical*
  CD102--CD152 (the complete operas, singspiels, serenatas), plus CD182--CD183 (Acis), CD188--CD191 (operatic arrangements)

- *Eclectic/Various*
  CD174--CD178 (fragments, completions), CD179--CD181 (arrangements from other composers), etc.
