ICLR 2014 shows growth in physics-inspired approaches with the emergence of variational autoencoders (VAEs) as a major theme. The conference demonstrates increasing sophistication in optimization methods and the first signs of what will become geometric deep learning.

Compared to 2013:
- Continued strength in variational methods
- Emergence of more sophisticated generative models
- Growing interest in sparse representations
- First appearance of papers analyzing deep networks from theoretical physics perspectives

* Key Insights for 2014
- VAEs emerge as a fundamental contribution (Auto-Encoding Variational Bayes)
- Variational methods dominate (36.8% of papers, up from 29.4%)
- First geometric analysis of neural networks appears
- Energy-based perspective remains limited but present
- Sparse methods gain prominence
- Year-over-year growth: +11.8% in physics-related papers
