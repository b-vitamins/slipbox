ICLR 2019 marks explosive growth with the formal emergence of equivariant neural networks, first diffusion-related methods, and PDEs for neural networks. This represents the largest absolute increase in physics-related papers and shows the field reaching maturity.

Compared to previous years:
- Largest growth in conference history (+60%)
- Equivariant models emerge as major category (7 papers)
- First diffusion-related paper (Langevin diffusion)
- First explicit PDE solver with neural networks
- Optimal transport continues strong presence
- Mean field theory applications expand
- Gauge theory enters ML (Gauge Equivariant Mesh CNNs)
