ICLR 2020 sees the emergence of molecular applications as a distinct category and continued strength in equivariant models. Energy-based models experience a renaissance, and Hamiltonian/Lagrangian neural networks become more sophisticated. SE(3) equivariance becomes a key theme.

Compared to previous years:
- Molecular applications emerge (2 papers)
- Energy-based models resurge (9 papers, 15.8%)
- Equivariant models continue growth (9 papers)
- Hamiltonian/Lagrangian frameworks mature
- Physics-as-inverse-graphics introduced
- Quantum methods return (2 papers)
- More focus on 3D geometry and SE(3) symmetries
