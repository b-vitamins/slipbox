ICLR 2022 represents peak growth with physics-inspired methods now mainstream. Diffusion models mature rapidly, equivariant models dominate geometric learning, molecular applications explode, and physics-informed neural networks establish as a major category. This is the highest paper count in conference history.

Compared to previous years:
- Highest absolute paper count (127 papers)
- Diffusion models mature with 10 papers
- Equivariant models reach 29 papers (22.8%)
- PDEs/Physics-informed methods explode (15 papers)
- Molecular applications grow 7.5x (15 papers)
- Every physics category well-represented
- Quantum methods stabilize (2 papers)
