#+TITLE: Off-Critical Correlations
#+FILETAGS: :physics:spof:ch4:correlations:

Off-critical correlations exhibit the Ornstein-Zernike form with exponential cutoff at the correlation length, smoothly interpolating between power-law critical behavior and exponential decay in the ordered or disordered phases.

In the scaling limit where t → 0 and |x| → ∞ with |x|/ξ fixed, the correlation function takes the homogeneous form G(x, t) = |x|^(-(d-2+η)) g(|x|/ξ), where the scaling function g(z) interpolates between g(0) = const and g(z→∞) ~ z^((d-2+η)/2) e^(-z). This scaling form encapsulates the full temperature dependence of correlations near criticality and can be tested directly in scattering experiments through the momentum-dependent structure factor.

The crossover from critical power-law behavior at short distances to exponential decay at distances comparable to the correlation length provides a direct experimental signature of the finite correlation length and its divergence as criticality is approached.
