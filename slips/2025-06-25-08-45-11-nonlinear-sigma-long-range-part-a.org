* Problem Statement

The long-range interaction, $K(x)$, is the Fourier transform of $K q^{\omega} / 2$ with $\omega<2$. What kind of asymptotic decay of interactions at long distances is consistent with such decay? (Dimensional analysis is sufficient for the answer, and no explicit integrations are required.)

* Context

This is part (a) of problem 8-5 on Nonlinear sigma model with long-range interactions from Kardar's Statistical Physics of Fields.

The Hamiltonian is:
\begin{align*}
\beta \mathcal{H}=\int \mathrm{d}^{d} \mathbf{x} \int \mathrm{d}^{d} \mathbf{y} K(|\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{y}|) \vec{s}(\mathbf{x}) \cdot \vec{s}(\mathbf{y})
\end{align*}

where $\vec{s}(\mathbf{x})$ are unit $n$-component spins with $|\vec{s}(\mathbf{x})|^{2}=1$.
