#+TITLE: RG in Momentum Space
#+FILETAGS: :physics:statistical:fieldtheory:renormalization:momentum:

The choice z = b^(1+d/2) keeps the gradient term fixed (K' = K), establishing the Gaussian fixed point. This determines the eigenvalues y_t = 2 and y_h = 1 + d/2, from which all critical exponents follow. The momentum space RG clearly shows why the Gaussian fixed point is stable above d = 4: all interaction terms have negative scaling dimensions and flow to zero.

Below four dimensions, the quartic interaction becomes relevant, signaling the need for a non-perturbative treatment. The momentum space formulation thus provides both a practical calculational tool and deep insight into the dimensional dependence of critical phenomena.
