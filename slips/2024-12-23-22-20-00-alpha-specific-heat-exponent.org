#+TITLE: α (Specific Heat Exponent)
#+FILETAGS: :physics:statistical:exponents:specificheat:alpha:
#+SETUPFILE: ~/.config/emacs/setup/setupfile.org

The specific heat exponent α controls the divergence of heat capacity at criticality: C ~ |t|^(-α).

In the Gaussian model, α = 2 - d/2, giving divergence for d < 4 and finite limits for d > 4. Real systems in three dimensions typically have small positive α (weak divergence) or small negative α (cusp), depending on the universality class.

The specific heat exponent is particularly sensitive to the spatial dimension and the number of order parameter components, making it a discriminating test of theoretical predictions. The sign change at d = 4 signals the special role of the upper critical dimension.
