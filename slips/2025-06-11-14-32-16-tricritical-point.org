#+TITLE: Tricritical point
#+FILETAGS: :problem:main:spof:
#+SETUPFILE: ~/.config/emacs/setup/setupfile.org

Tricritical point: By tuning an additional parameter, a second order transition can be made first order. The special point separating the two types of transitions is known as a tricritical point, and can be studied by examining the Landau-Ginzburg Hamiltonian

\begin{align*}
\beta \mathcal{H}=\int \mathrm{d}^{d} \mathbf{x}\left
[
\frac{K}{2}(\nabla m)^{2}+\frac{t}{2} m^{2}+u m^{4}+v m^{6}-h m\right]
\end{align*}

where \(u\) can be positive or negative. For \(u<0\), a positive \(v\) is necessary to insure stability.
