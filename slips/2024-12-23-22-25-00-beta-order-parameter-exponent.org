#+TITLE: β (Order Parameter Exponent)
#+FILETAGS: :physics:statistical:exponents:orderparameter:beta:
#+SETUPFILE: ~/.config/emacs/setup/setupfile.org

The order parameter exponent β controls the approach to order: m ~ |t|^β for t < 0.

The exponent β relates intimately to the correlation length exponent through hyperscaling: β = ν(d - 2 + η)/2. This shows that β vanishes at d = 2 - η, marking the lower critical dimension below which continuous symmetry breaking is impossible (Mermin-Wagner theorem).

The measurement of β through neutron scattering, magnetization, or other order parameter probes provides one of the most direct experimental determinations of critical exponents. The universality of β across different physical systems demonstrates the power of the scaling hypothesis.
