#+TITLE: XY Model (n=2) Exponents
#+FILETAGS: :physics:statistical:xy:exponents:continuous:
#+SETUPFILE: ~/.config/emacs/setup/setupfile.org

Critical exponents for the 3D XY universality class:
- β = 0.35 (order parameter)
- γ = 1.32 (susceptibility)  
- δ = 4.7 (critical isotherm)
- ν = 0.67 (correlation length)
- η = 0.04 (anomalous dimension)

Physical realizations include superfluid helium-4 transition, easy-plane ferromagnets, Josephson junction arrays, and certain structural phase transitions.

Notable features:
- Nearly zero α indicates logarithmic specific heat divergence
- Larger ν than Ising reflects enhanced fluctuations from continuous symmetry
- η remains small, similar to Ising case
- β and γ slightly larger than Ising values

The XY model in 2D exhibits the Kosterlitz-Thouless transition, fundamentally different from conventional critical phenomena. In 3D, the transition is conventional but with enhanced fluctuations due to the continuous O(2) symmetry.
