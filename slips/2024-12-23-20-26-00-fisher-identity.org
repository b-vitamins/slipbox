#+TITLE: Fisher Identity
#+FILETAGS: :physics:spof:ch4:correlations:

The Fisher identity γ = (2-η)ν connects the susceptibility exponent γ to the correlation length exponent ν and the anomalous dimension η, emerging from the spatial integration of correlation functions.

The identity arises from the fluctuation-dissipation relation χ = ∫d^d x G(x) and the power-law form of correlations G(x) ~ |x|^(-(d-2+η)) at criticality. The spatial integral converges at the correlation length scale, yielding χ ~ ξ^(2-η) ~ |t|^(-ν(2-η)).

This fundamental relation bridges thermodynamic response functions and spatial correlation structure, demonstrating how macroscopic susceptibility emerges from microscopic correlations.
