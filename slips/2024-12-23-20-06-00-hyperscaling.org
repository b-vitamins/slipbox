#+TITLE: Hyperscaling
#+FILETAGS: :physics:spof:ch4:scaling:
#+SETUPFILE: ~/.config/emacs/setup/setupfile.org

Hyperscaling relations connect critical exponents to the spatial dimension d, providing additional constraints beyond the standard scaling relations. The key hyperscaling relation is 2 - α = dν, connecting the specific heat exponent α to the correlation length exponent ν through the dimensionality.

These relations are valid below the upper critical dimension d_c = 4, where fluctuations dominate over mean-field behavior. Above d_c, hyperscaling breaks down as mean-field theory becomes exact and fluctuation corrections vanish.

Hyperscaling emerges from the physical requirement that the free energy density must scale properly with the correlation length: f_sing ~ ξ^(-d). This geometric constraint links thermodynamic and spatial properties of critical systems.
