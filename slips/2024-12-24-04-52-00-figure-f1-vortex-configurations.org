**Sketch Description:**
Two elementary vortices in the XY model, showing spin orientations around n = +1 (left) and n = -1 (right) defects.

**Left vortex (n = +1):** 
Center point marked with ⊕. Surrounding spins arranged in circle, arrows showing counterclockwise rotation:
- Top: ↑
- Right: →
- Bottom: ↓  
- Left: ←
- Intermediate angles interpolate smoothly

**Right vortex (n = -1):**
Center point marked with ⊖. Surrounding spins arranged in circle, arrows showing clockwise rotation:
- Top: ↑
- Right: ←
- Bottom: ↓
- Left: →
- Intermediate angles interpolate smoothly

**Key features:** The angle θ increases by 2π going counterclockwise around the positive vortex, decreases by 2π around the negative vortex. Far from the core, ∇θ = ±θ̂/r where θ̂ is the azimuthal unit vector. The singularity at the core (where θ is undefined) is regularized by the lattice structure over distance ~a.

**Physical significance:** These topological defects cannot be removed by smooth deformations. A positive and negative vortex can annihilate if brought together, but isolated vortices are topologically stable. Their unbinding at the Kosterlitz-Thouless transition destroys quasi-long-range order.
