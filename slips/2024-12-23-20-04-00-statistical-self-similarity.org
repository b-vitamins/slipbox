#+TITLE: Statistical Self-Similarity
#+FILETAGS: :physics:spof:ch4:fractals:
#+SETUPFILE: ~/.config/emacs/setup/setupfile.org

Critical systems exhibit statistical self-similarity, meaning they look the same at all length scales. This fractal property is mathematically expressed through the scaling relation G(λx) = λ^p G(x), where G is a correlation function and p is the scaling dimension.

Self-similarity emerges because the [[id:b41e2d14-3737-4c1d-b87e-bd501a46ddeb][Correlation Length as Master Scale]] diverges at criticality, eliminating any characteristic length scale. Without a natural scale, the system appears identical under scale transformations.

This property underlies the success of the renormalization group: coarse-graining operations preserve the statistical structure of critical systems, allowing systematic analysis of scale-invariant physics through iterative transformations.
