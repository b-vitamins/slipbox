#+TITLE: Homogeneity Assumption
#+FILETAGS: :physics:spof:ch4:scaling:
#+SETUPFILE: ~/.config/emacs/setup/setupfile.org

The homogeneity assumption is the foundational principle underlying all critical phenomena scaling theory. It posits that at criticality, physical quantities exhibit power-law scaling behavior with characteristic exponents.

This assumption leads directly to the scaling hypothesis: thermodynamic functions become homogeneous functions of their arguments near the critical point, allowing the extraction of universal critical exponents through dimensional analysis.

The mathematical formalization appears in [[id:1bb44a5f-852b-4fca-8634-3e3746cc9a1a][Homogeneity Mathematical Framework]], where all scaling forms are systematically derived from this single principle.
