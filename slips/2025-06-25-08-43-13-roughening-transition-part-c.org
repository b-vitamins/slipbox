* Problem Statement

Use the results in (a) to calculate $G_{k}(\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{y})$ in perturbation theory to order of $y_{0}^{2}$. (Hint: Set $\cos (2 \pi h)=\left(\mathrm{e}^{2 \mathrm{i} \pi h}+\mathrm{e}^{-2 \mathrm{i} \pi h}\right) / 2$. The first order terms vanish according to the result in (a), while the second order contribution is identical in structure to that of the Coulomb gas described in this chapter.)

* Context

This is part (c) of problem 8-3 on The roughening transition from Kardar's Statistical Physics of Fields.

The perturbation term is:
\begin{align*}
-\beta U=y_{0} \int \mathrm{d}^{2} \mathbf{x} \cos (2 \pi h)
\end{align*}
