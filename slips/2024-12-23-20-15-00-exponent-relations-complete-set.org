#+TITLE: Exponent Relations Complete Set
#+FILETAGS: :physics:spof:ch4:exponents:

The complete set of exponent relations emerges from the thermodynamic derivatives of the scaling form, providing fundamental constraints on critical behavior.

**Key definitions**: β = 2-α-Δ (Equation 4.13, from m ~ |t|^β at h=0)

δ = Δ/β = Δ/(2-α-Δ) (Equation 4.15, from m ~ h^(1/δ) at t=0)

γ = 2Δ - 2 + α (Equation 4.16, from χ ~ |t|^(-γ) at h=0)

**Classical exponent identities**:

*Rushbrooke's identity*: α + 2β + γ = α + 2(2-α-Δ) + (2Δ-2+α) = 2 (Equation 4.17)

*Widom's identity*: δ - 1 = Δ/(2-α-Δ) - 1 = (2Δ-2+α)/(2-α-Δ) = γ/β (Equation 4.17)

These identities serve as crucial consistency checks for experimental and theoretical determinations of critical exponents. Their universal validity across diverse physical systems provides the strongest evidence for the scaling hypothesis.
