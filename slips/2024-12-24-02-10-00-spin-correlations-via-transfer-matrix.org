#+TITLE: Spin Correlations via Transfer Matrix
#+FILETAGS: :physics:spof:ch6:transfer:matrix:correlations:
#+SETUPFILE: ~/.config/emacs/setup/setupfile.org

Correlation functions emerge from transfer matrix formalism through operator insertions that probe local spin configurations. The single-spin expectation value requires evaluating

\[\langle\sigma_i\rangle = \frac{1}{Z}\sum_{\sigma_1=\pm 1}\sum_{\sigma_2=\pm 1}\cdots\sum_{\sigma_N=\pm 1}\sigma_i\prod_{j=1}^N\exp(K\sigma_j\sigma_{j+1})\]

Matrix notation transforms this into

\[\langle\sigma_i\rangle = \frac{1}{Z}\operatorname{tr}\left[T^{i-1}\hat{\sigma}_z T^{N-i+1}\right] = \frac{1}{Z}\operatorname{tr}\left[T^N\hat{\sigma}_z\right]\]

where cyclicity of the trace allows temporal translation. The Pauli matrix \(\hat{\sigma}_z = \begin{pmatrix}1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1\end{pmatrix}\) encodes the spin observable. For h=0, diagonalization via \(U = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\begin{pmatrix}1 & 1 \\ 1 & -1\end{pmatrix}\) transforms \(\hat{\sigma}_z \to \hat{\sigma}_x = \begin{pmatrix}0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0\end{pmatrix}\), yielding

\[\langle\sigma_i\rangle = \frac{1}{Z}\operatorname{tr}\begin{pmatrix}\lambda_+^N & 0 \\ 0 & \lambda_-^N\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix}0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0\end{pmatrix} = 0\]

The vanishing magnetization reflects unbroken Z₂ symmetry. Two-spin correlations require double operator insertion:

\[\langle\sigma_i\sigma_{i+r}\rangle = \frac{1}{Z}\operatorname{tr}\left[\hat{\sigma}_z T^r\hat{\sigma}_z T^{N-r}\right]\]

Diagonalization yields

\[\langle\sigma_i\sigma_{i+r}\rangle = \frac{\lambda_+^{N-r}\lambda_-^r + \lambda_-^{N-r}\lambda_+^r}{\lambda_+^N + \lambda_-^N}\]

For N≫r, this reduces to pure exponential decay.

The transfer matrix correlation calculation exemplifies a general principle: observables become operator insertions in the trace, with time evolution generated by matrix powers. This quantum mechanical language—states, operators, time evolution—permeates statistical mechanics, culminating in the path integral formulation where d-dimensional classical systems map to (d-1)-dimensional quantum systems. The operator insertion technique extends to arbitrary correlations, though multi-point functions rapidly become algebraically complex. The elegance lies in reducing statistical sums over 2^N configurations to linear algebra in 2×2 matrices.
