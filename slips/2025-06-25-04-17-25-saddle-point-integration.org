* Part (c) Problem
By saddle point integration show that the actual free energy \(F(h, T)=\) \(-k_{\mathrm{B}} T \ln Z(h, T)\) is given by \(F(h, T)=\min [F(m, h)]_{m}\). When is the saddle point method valid? Note that \(F(m, h)\) is an analytic function but not convex for \(T<T_{c}\), while the true free energy \(F(h, T)\) is convex but becomes non-analytic due to the minimization.
