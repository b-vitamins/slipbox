#+title: Figure F4: Dislocation Geometry

#+SETUPFILE: ~/.config/emacs/setup/setupfile.org

**Sketch Description:**
Edge dislocations in triangular and square lattices showing extra half-planes and Burgers circuits.

**Triangular lattice (top):**
- Regular triangular array of atoms (dots)
- Extra half-row of atoms inserted from top, ending at dislocation core (marked ⊥)
- Burgers circuit: hexagonal path around dislocation
  - Start at atom A, make closed path in perfect lattice
  - Path fails to close by vector **b** (shown as arrow)
  - |**b**| = a (lattice constant)
  - Six possible directions for **b** (nearest neighbors)

**Square lattice (bottom):**
- Regular square array of atoms
- Extra half-column inserted from left, ending at core (⊥)
- Burgers circuit: rectangular path around dislocation
  - Closure failure gives **b** pointing right
  - |**b**| = a
  - Four possible directions for **b**

**Key features:**
The displacement field must be multivalued: ∮∇**u**·d**s** = **b**. Near the core (~a), continuum elasticity breaks down. Far from core, elastic distortions decay as 1/r. The Burgers vector is quantized in units of lattice vectors, making dislocations topologically stable.

**Physical significance:** Dislocations mediate plastic deformation and, in 2D, drive the solid-hexatic transition by unbinding at T₁.
