#+TITLE: Singular Part Extraction
#+FILETAGS: :physics:statistical:fieldtheory:singularities:extraction:

This extraction procedure illustrates general principles for identifying critical singularities. First, singularities arise from infrared physics—the small-momentum, long-wavelength modes. Second, appropriate scaling variables must be introduced to make the t-dependence manifest. Third, the singular part typically emerges from the asymptotic behavior of integrals as scaling variables approach limits.

The same principles apply beyond the Gaussian model, though the technical implementation becomes considerably more complex when interactions are present. The successful extraction of f_sing ~ t^(2-α) with α = 2 - d/2 confirms the hyperscaling relation at the Gaussian level.
