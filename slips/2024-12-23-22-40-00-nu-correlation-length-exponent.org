#+TITLE: ν (Correlation Length Exponent)
#+FILETAGS: :physics:statistical:exponents:correlation:nu:
#+SETUPFILE: ~/.config/emacs/setup/setupfile.org

The correlation length exponent ν controls the divergence of the characteristic length scale: ξ ~ |t|^(-ν).

The experimental determination of ν often proceeds indirectly through scattering experiments that measure the correlation function, or through finite-size scaling where system properties depend on L/ξ. The universality of ν implies that correlation lengths diverge identically (up to non-universal amplitudes) in systems as different as magnetic materials, binary fluids, and superfluid transitions.

This universal divergence of spatial correlations represents perhaps the most fundamental signature of criticality. The correlation length serves as the natural unit for all length scales in the critical region, making ν a central quantity in the scaling description.
