* Part (b) Problem
For each cell \(\alpha\), define a renormalized spin \(\sigma_{\alpha}^{\prime}=\operatorname{sign}\left(\sigma_{\alpha}^{1}+\sigma_{\alpha}^{2}+\sigma_{\alpha}^{3}+\sigma_{\alpha}^{4}\right)\). This choice becomes ambiguous for configurations such that \(\sum_{i=1}^{4} \sigma_{\alpha}^{i}=0\). Distribute the weight of these configurations equally between \(\sigma_{\alpha}^{\prime}=+1\) and -1 (i.e. put a factor of \(1 / 2\) in addition to the Boltzmann weight). Make a table for all possible configurations of a cell, the internal probability \(\exp \left(-\beta \mathcal{H}_{0}\right)\), and the weights contributing to \(\sigma_{\alpha}^{\prime}= \pm 1\).
