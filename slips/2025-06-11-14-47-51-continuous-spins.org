#+TITLE: Continuous spins
#+FILETAGS: :problem:main:spof:
#+SETUPFILE: ../../setupfile.org

Continuous spins: In the standard \(\mathcal{O}(n)\) model, n component unit vectors are placed on the sites of a lattice. The nearest-neighbor spins are then connected by a bond
J \vec{s}_{i} \cdot \vec{s}_{j}. In fact, if we are only interested in universal properties, any generalized interaction f(\vec{s}_{i} \cdot \vec{s}_{j}) leads to the same critical behavior. By analogy with the Ising model, a suitable choice is

\begin{align*}
\exp \left
[
f\left(\vec{s}_{i} \cdot \vec{s}_{j}\right)\right]=1+(n t) \vec{s}_{i} \cdot \vec{s}_{j}
\end{align*}

resulting in the so called loop model.
