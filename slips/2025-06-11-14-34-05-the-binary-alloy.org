#+TITLE: The binary alloy
#+FILETAGS: :problem:main:spof:
#+SETUPFILE: ../../setupfile.org

The binary alloy: A binary alloy (as in \(\beta\) brass) consists of \(N_{A}\) atoms of type \(A\), and \(N_{B}\) atoms of type \(B\). The atoms form a simple cubic lattice, each interacting only with its six nearest neighbors. Assume an attractive energy of \(-J \quad(J>0)\) between like neighbors \(A-A\) and \(B-B\), but a repulsive energy of \(+J\) for an \(A-B\) pair.
