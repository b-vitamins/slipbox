#+TITLE: Josephson Identity
#+FILETAGS: :physics:spof:ch4:hyperscaling:

The Josephson identity 2 - α = dν establishes the crucial hyperscaling relation connecting the specific heat exponent α to the correlation length exponent ν through the spatial dimension d.

This relation emerges from dimensional analysis of the free energy density in terms of the correlation length: f_sing ~ ξ^(-d) ~ |t|^(dν). Combined with the scaling form f_sing ~ |t|^(2-α), this directly yields the Josephson identity.

The identity is fundamental to hyperscaling theory and is valid below the upper critical dimension d_c = 4. Above d_c, the identity breaks down as mean-field theory becomes exact and fluctuation effects vanish.
