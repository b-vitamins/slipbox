#+TITLE: Heisenberg Model (n=3) Exponents
#+FILETAGS: :physics:statistical:heisenberg:exponents:o3:
#+SETUPFILE: ~/.config/emacs/setup/setupfile.org

Critical exponents for the 3D Heisenberg universality class:
- β = 0.36 (order parameter)
- γ = 1.39 (susceptibility)
- δ = 4.9 (critical isotherm)
- ν = 0.70 (correlation length)
- η = 0.04 (anomalous dimension)

Physical realizations include isotropic ferromagnets (EuO, EuS, RbMnF₃), antiferromagnets with weak anisotropy, and some structural transitions with O(3) symmetry.

Systematic trends with n:
- α decreases: 0.11 (n=1) → −0.01 (n=2) → −0.11 (n=3)
- ν increases: 0.63 → 0.67 → 0.70
- γ increases: 1.24 → 1.32 → 1.39
- η remains nearly constant ≈ 0.04

The continuous O(3) symmetry allows for Goldstone modes in the ordered phase, enhancing critical fluctuations. The systematic increase in ν with n reflects the growing phase space for fluctuations as the order parameter components increase.
