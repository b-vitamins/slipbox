* Part (b) Problem
Regard the quadratic terms in \(\phi_{\ell}\) and \(\vec{\phi}_{t}\) as an unperturbed Hamiltonian \(\beta \mathcal{H}_{0}\), and the lowest order term coupling \(\phi_{\ell}\) and \(\vec{\phi}_{t}\) as a perturbation \(U\); i.e.

\begin{align*}
U=4 u \bar{m} \int \mathrm{d}^{d} \mathbf{x} \phi_{\ell}(\mathbf{x}) \vec{\phi}_{t}(\mathbf{x})^{2}
\end{align*}

Write \(U\) in Fourier space in terms of \(\phi_{\ell}(\mathbf{q})\) and \(\vec{\phi}_{t}(\mathbf{q})\).
