#+TITLE: Gap Exponent
#+FILETAGS: :physics:spof:ch4:exponents:

The gap exponent Δ is the universal ratio that controls field scaling across all thermodynamic quantities near the critical point. It determines how the magnetic field h scales relative to the reduced temperature t in the scaling hypothesis.

Defined through the relation Δ = βδ, where β is the order parameter exponent and δ is the critical isotherm exponent. The gap exponent appears in all homogeneous scaling forms, controlling the relative importance of field versus temperature deviations from criticality.

This universal ratio emerges from the [[id:aaeee0ab-91d5-4186-a7b7-8ad92572f72f][Homogeneity Assumption]] and is systematically derived in [[id:1bb44a5f-852b-4fca-8634-3e3746cc9a1a][Homogeneity Mathematical Framework]].
