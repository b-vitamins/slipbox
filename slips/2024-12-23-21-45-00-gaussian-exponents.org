These mean-field exponents become exact above the upper critical dimension d = 4, where the quartic interaction is irrelevant. In physical dimensions d = 2 and d = 3, the Gaussian exponents provide a reference point, with real exponents deviating due to the relevant φ⁴ interaction.

The dimensional dependence of Gaussian exponents—particularly α changing sign at d = 4—signals the special role of four dimensions. Below d = 4, α < 0 implies a specific heat divergence, while above, α > 0 means the specific heat approaches a finite limit, though with a possible jump discontinuity at d = 4 where logarithmic corrections appear.
