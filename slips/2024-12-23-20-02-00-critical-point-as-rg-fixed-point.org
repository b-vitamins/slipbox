#+TITLE: Critical Point as RG Fixed Point
#+FILETAGS: :physics:spof:ch4:rg:

The critical point corresponds to a fixed point of the renormalization group transformation, characterized by the invariance condition S* = ℜ_b S*. This means scale-invariant theories remain unchanged under coarse-graining operations.

At the fixed point, all length scales are equivalent, leading to power-law correlations without characteristic scale. The critical point's universal properties emerge from this scale invariance, independent of microscopic details.

The fixed point structure explains universality: different microscopic systems flow to the same infrared fixed point, yielding identical critical exponents. This provides the theoretical foundation for understanding why diverse physical systems exhibit the same critical behavior.
