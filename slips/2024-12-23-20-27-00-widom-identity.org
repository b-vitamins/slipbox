#+TITLE: Widom Identity
#+FILETAGS: :physics:spof:ch4:exponents:

The Widom identity δ - 1 = γ/β relates the critical isotherm exponent δ to the ratio of susceptibility and order parameter exponents, expressing the constraint between field and temperature scaling.

Using the definitions δ = Δ/β and γ = 2Δ - 2 + α, we can derive: δ - 1 = Δ/β - 1 = (2Δ - 2 + α)/(2 - α - Δ) = γ/β, where we used γ = 2Δ - 2 + α. This relation constrains the shape of the critical isotherm relative to the temperature dependence of the order parameter and susceptibility.

Widom's identity played a crucial historical role in the development of scaling theory, as it was one of the first exact relations among critical exponents to be experimentally verified across diverse systems.
