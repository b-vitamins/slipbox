#+TITLE: Why Gaussian Theory Fails
#+FILETAGS: :physics:statistical:fieldtheory:gaussian:failure:
#+SETUPFILE: ~/.config/emacs/setup/setupfile.org

The failure of Gaussian theory below four dimensions necessitates finding a new fixed point where the quartic interaction takes a finite, non-zero value. This Wilson-Fisher fixed point, accessible through ε = 4 - d expansion, controls the true critical behavior in physical dimensions.

The crossover at d = 4 from Gaussian to non-Gaussian behavior represents one of the most important results in critical phenomena, explaining why mean-field theory fails for most real phase transitions while becoming exact in high-dimensional or long-range systems.

The quartic term becomes relevant (grows under RG flow) when its scaling dimension becomes positive, which occurs precisely at d = 4. Below this dimension, interactions fundamentally alter the critical behavior from the Gaussian fixed point predictions.
