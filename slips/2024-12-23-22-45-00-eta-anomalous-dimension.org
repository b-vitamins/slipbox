#+TITLE: η (Anomalous Dimension)
#+FILETAGS: :physics:statistical:exponents:anomalous:eta:
#+SETUPFILE: ~/.config/emacs/setup/setupfile.org

The anomalous dimension η modifies the classical correlation decay: G(x) ~ 1/|x|^(d-2+η).

In momentum space, the anomalous dimension manifests as G(q) ~ 1/q^(2-η) for small q, directly measurable in scattering experiments. The structure factor at small momentum thus provides S(q) ~ q^(-(2-η)), with deviations from q^(-2) signaling the presence of anomalous scaling.

The emergence of non-zero η from relevant interactions marks the breakdown of Gaussian theory and the onset of truly critical behavior. Even the small values observed in three dimensions represent a qualitative departure from mean-field predictions. The anomalous dimension quantifies how interactions modify the naive dimensional analysis.
