#+TITLE: Gaussian Fixed Point
#+FILETAGS: :physics:spof:ch4:gaussian:
#+SETUPFILE: ~/.config/emacs/setup/setupfile.org

The Gaussian fixed point βℋ* = K/2 ∫d^d x (∇m)² represents the scale-invariant quadratic theory that serves as the foundation for understanding critical phenomena in the mean-field regime.

The physical significance of the Gaussian fixed point extends beyond its mathematical simplicity. It describes systems where fluctuations are weak and uncorrelated beyond a microscopic scale, corresponding to mean-field behavior. Above four dimensions, critical phenomena are essentially mean-field-like on scales larger than the Ginzburg length, with logarithmic corrections at exactly d = 4.

The Gaussian fixed point is stable for d > 4 but becomes unstable to quartic perturbations for d < 4, where fluctuations dominate and drive the system to a non-Gaussian fixed point. This instability marks the breakdown of mean-field theory in physical dimensions.
