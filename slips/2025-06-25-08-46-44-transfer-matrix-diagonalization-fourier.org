* Part (b) Problem
Write down the transfer matrix explicitly and diagonalize it. Note that you do not have to solve a \(q\) th order secular equation; because of the translational symmetry, the eigenvalues are easily obtained by discrete Fourier transformation as

\[\lambda(k)=\sum_{n=1}^{q} \exp \left[J(n)+\frac{2 \pi \mathrm{i} n k}{q}\right]\]
